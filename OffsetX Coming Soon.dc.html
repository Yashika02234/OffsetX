03 — THE PROBLEM

The carbon market is scaling without the infrastructure it needs.

FRAGMENTATION
No unified carbon data layer
Carbon data is scattered across dozens of registries, methodologies, and geographies. There is no consolidated, machine-readable view of the global market.
VERIFICATION GAP
No standard for credit quality
No standardised way to assess carbon credit quality across registries and jurisdictions. Buyers cannot independently verify what they're purchasing.
INFRASTRUCTURE
No universal trust layer
No universal trust layer exists across carbon registries, markets, and geographies. The market is scaling without the infrastructure it needs to sustain that growth.
COMPLIANCE OPACITY
Regulatory exposure is opaque
Organisations cannot easily map their exposure under CBAM, Article 6, and evolving national frameworks. Compliance risk is invisible until it's urgent.
TRUST DEFICIT
Greenwashing eroded confidence
Greenwashing scandals have damaged market credibility. Buyers can no longer verify what they are paying for without third-party tooling that doesn't yet exist.
MARKET INTELLIGENCE
Pricing and supply data is fragmented
Real-time data on supply, demand, pricing, and registry activity is fragmented across proprietary sources with no unified, structured access layer.
